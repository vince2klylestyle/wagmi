# Kilroy × Motion-Tier Craft Bridge

> **Read this first if you're about to write a Kilroy brief.** This
> playbook is what separates motion-tier Kilroy pieces from lazy
> Kilroy stickers. The base photo should look like a $MOTION piece.
> The Kilroy graffiti is the signature on top.

## The thesis

A bad Kilroy piece is: clipart Kilroy dropped onto a random stock
photo, rendered at 2K with no craft discipline.

A motion-tier Kilroy piece is: a **real film still / archival photo /
VHS rip / polaroid** treated with the same craft discipline $MOTION
applies (named lens, named film stock, named lighting, letterbox 9:16,
serif title card when appropriate, visible grain), with a **small
chalk Kilroy in the corner** as the signature.

The Kilroy piece should feel like a motion piece that happens to
carry a graffito. Not like a Kilroy sticker with a photo behind it.

## What to steal from $MOTION

1. **Named lens + film stock.** "Arri Alexa 65 / Panavision anamorphic /
   35mm Tri-X pushed one stop." NEVER "cinematic." Specificity is the
   difference between motion and amateur.
2. **Letterbox discipline.** Vertical 9:16 canvas with clean black bars
   top and bottom holding a cinema-aspect (2.39:1) frame in the middle.
   Phone frame holding a cinema frame — the motion signature.
3. **Serif title card (selectively).** When the piece needs a title,
   use Trajan / Didot / Bodoni in white, all-caps, letter-spaced wide,
   centered. Treat it like a film poster title, not an overlay.
4. **B&W / crushed-black grade.** Motion runs 40-50% monochrome.
   Kilroy should lean even harder into B&W — press-photo + chalk
   graffiti is already a black-and-white story.
5. **Grain + halation.** Never clean digital. If the scene is digital-
   capture, post-grain it to 35mm Tri-X or Plus-X levels. If it's
   period, add halation on bright highlights.
6. **No stock-photo lighting.** Pick a named setup: press flash with
   halation, hard window light at 3:1, single-source tungsten at 3200K
   with deep shadow fall-off. Stock-photo even lighting kills the piece.

## What stays pure Kilroy

1. **The character's rules don't bend.** Bald, long nose, four fingers,
   one continuous line, chalk or sharpie, always a corner accent.
2. **"KILROY WAS HERE" block caps** below the figure, hand-lettered,
   never a clean font.
3. **Placement is always corner / edge.** Even in a motion-grade
   cinema still, Kilroy is a sub-15% corner element.
4. **Never color.** The tag stays pure white chalk on dark, or pure
   black sharpie on light. Never tinted, never toned.
5. **Kilroy is never the subject.** The cinema craft is the subject;
   Kilroy is the signature proving he was there first.

## The four canonical Kilroy pieces (motion-tier)

### 1. Cinema film still + chalk Kilroy
A frame that could plausibly appear in *The Godfather* / *Heat* /
*Blade Runner* / *Collateral* — named lens, named film stock, named
lighting, letterboxed 9:16. Kilroy in the corner on a visible wall /
door / prop. The viewer should recognize the CRAFT before they notice
the graffiti.

### 2. VHS / broadcast rip + chalk Kilroy
A 480p interlaced scanline-heavy broadcast still from 1987-1995 (news
anchor, stock-ticker chyron, sports broadcast, infomercial). Kilroy
chalk on the stall door visible in the background, or sharpie on the
monitor bezel. Reads as a tape capture, not an AI render.

### 3. Tabloid cover / magazine spread + chalk Kilroy
National Enquirer / New York Post / Time / Wired cover treatment —
masthead, red slab headline, halftone-printed celebrity photo.
Kilroy tag added in the lower corner as if drawn on the printed page.

### 4. Polaroid stack + chalk Kilroy
A stack of Polaroid SX-70 photos on a surface, lit from above, one
on top showing the scene. Kilroy drawn in sharpie directly on the
white border of the top polaroid, "KILROY WAS HERE" in the caption
zone at the bottom of the border.

## Motion-tier Kilroy fragments (use these)

Reference with `KILROY.*` in any prompt:

- `KILROY.peek_figure` — the character itself (invariants)
- `KILROY.chalk_tag` — the "KILROY WAS HERE" legend
- `KILROY.corner_placement` — placement rule
- `KILROY.cinema_letterbox_916` — motion-grade 9:16 cinema frame
- `KILROY.serif_title_card` — optional Trajan/Didot film-title layer
- `KILROY.named_film_stock` — explicit film-stock specifier options
- `KILROY.named_lighting_setup` — named lighting options
- `KILROY.grain_and_halation` — grain + halation treatment
- `KILROY.found_news_photo` — archival press-photo base
- `KILROY.crypto_terminal_tag` — 3am terminal variant
- `KILROY.gi_crayon_lettering` — the typography of the tag
- `KILROY.potsdam_bathroom` — lore reference
- `KILROY.fore_river_hull` — lore reference
- `KILROY.wheat_paste_poster` — street-poster variant

## One-line rule

*Make the base photo feel like a motion piece. Add a chalk Kilroy in
the corner. Let the viewer discover the tag.*
